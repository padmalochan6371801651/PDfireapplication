About Puri jagannath temple

The Puri Jagannath Temple, located in the coastal town of Puri, Odisha, is one of the most revered Hindu temples in India. Dedicated to Lord Jagannath, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu, the temple is part of the Char Dham pilgrimage, which also includes Badrinath, Dwarka, and Rameswaram. Built in the 12th century by King Anantavarman Chodaganga Deva, the temple's architecture is a prime example of the Kalinga style. The towering Shikhar (Vimana) of the temple stands at 65 meters and is visible from miles away. The main deities worshipped in the temple are Lord Jagannath, his brother Balabhadra, and sister Subhadra. These deities are uniquely represented in wooden forms, unlike the typical stone idols seen in most temples. The temple is renowned for its annual Rath Yatra (Chariot Festival), where the deities are carried out of the temple on massive chariots, attracting millions of devotees worldwide. Non-Hindus are not permitted to enter the temple, but they can view it from a nearby platform. The Jagannath Temple is also known for its massive kitchen, which prepares the famous Mahaprasad, served to thousands of devotees daily, symbolizing community and devotion.

May the blessings of Lord Jagannath guide you on the path of righteousness.

Jagannath is a revered deity in Hinduism, worshiped primarily in the city of Puri, Odisha, at the famous Jagannath Temple. Lord Jagannath is considered a form of Lord Vishnu and more specifically an incarnation of Lord Krishna. He is depicted with large round eyes and a smiling face, representing compassion and love. The word "Jagannath" means "Lord of the Universe", symbolizing his universal significance. Jagannath is worshiped along with his siblings, Balabhadra (brother) and Goddess Subhadra (sister). Together, they reside on the sacred throne in the Puri temple, which is one of the holiest pilgrimage sites in India and one of the Char Dham (four most sacred places).

The Puri Rath Yatra is an iconic Hindu festival celebrated annually in the coastal town of Puri, Odisha. It marks the grand procession of Lord Jagannath, along with his siblings, Lord Balabhadra and Goddess Subhadra, in elaborately decorated wooden chariots. The deities are taken from the Jagannath Temple to the Gundicha Temple, symbolizing a spiritual journey. This vibrant festival attracts millions of devotees from around the world, who come to pull the massive chariots, believing it brings divine blessings. The Rath Yatra, celebrated for centuries, is a symbol of unity, devotion, and cultural grandeur in India.
